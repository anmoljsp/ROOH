Our Values


MISSION: Our mission is to provide a high quality education to all those who are unable to exercise that fundamental Right of Education due to financial and social constraints. We strive towards our goal of a Kurukshetra and a India where the RTE is realized in its true form.


VISION: Our vision is to admit the students into a full-fledged public school with modern amenities which is compliant to the needs of current technical scenario so that they can learn, grow, earn and break the vicious cycle of poverty. We also envision them to be active and diverse in extra co-currilar activities and to be able to learn and pursue their true passions.

The Young Ones

The smiles we work for!

Jasleen

Jasleen, an orphan girl who lost her parents to a tragic holocaust, has the ‘voice of a nightingale’ that has the capability to leave the audience spellbound and has commendable intelligence that can make adults wonder and that’s not all, she is a fascinating debater and as well. She also has an exceptional command over the language and can fluently speak English, Hindi and Punjabi. She has already won number of awards for the uncountable talents she has. She is currently preparing for her 12th Boards examination.

Dheeraj

A +1 student at Nivedita Public School, Dheeraj is a student with high intellectual abilities and also has a good sportsman spirit as well. He aspires to serve the nation by joining defence services one day but today, that’s almost a dream because he is ‘Economically Backward’. Living in a family where having a meal thrice a day is a big struggle, leaving better-paying jobs and chosing the meagre salary of a sepoy is just a dream. But with proper education, he has the skills to become a high - ranking officer in the armed forces.

Palak

Palak is a chirpy and enthusiastic student studying in VI standard. She is very intelligent and harbours brilliant thoughts. She has a dream to become a great doctor and protect the lives of people at this tender age, when children of her age are busy playing and having fun with their cellphones. Her father is a daily wage worker and is struggling hard to make their ends meet for just the basic needs. In such a case, he is unable to afford good quality education for her, the kind required to become a great doctor.
