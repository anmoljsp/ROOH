    Blood Donation Camp (April 2017)

    Rooh society, under the aegis guidance of Alumni Association, welcomes you all to donate blood in a Blood Donation Camp we are organizing on November 11, 2017 10:00 am onwards in Examination Hall(Ground Floor) and save lives. The Camp would be conducted by the highly professional and efficient team of Red Cross, Delhi with all the safety precautions and pre-requisite tests.
    Donating Blood has several benefits to everyone involved with it. The blood of donor is cleansed after donation with older impure blood replaced by fresher one. Additionally, it reduces the chances of kidney stones, cholestrol and high blood pressure.
    But the real benefits lie in the lives we save through this. Each unit of blood you donate can save upto 3 lives. Thousands of people, including the children suffering from Thallasemia, die daily because of lack of proper blood type and blood banks. But if more and more people come forward, atleast it won’t be the lack of resources responsible for someone’s unfortunate death.
    Donating blood requires following a few instructions as well. The blood must be fresh and unadulterated, for which we have to follow the given guidelines for our own health as well.
    DO’S
    1. Take a Heavy Morning Breakfast along with milk.
    2. Rest well after donating blood to let proper blood circulation to return.
    3. Take a good amount of fluids for 24 hours after the donation.
    4. Avoid smoking or consuming alcohol content till the next meal.
    5. Avoid sports and strenous exercises for a day.
    DONT’S
    1. Don’t consume alcohol content or smoke for a day before donating.
    2. You mustn’t be under some medication or carrying an infection with you.
    3. Never resume your daily routine too soon after having donated, take a proper rest for an hour instead.
    4. You can’t donate in case you have a chronic illness, are underweight or having high blood pressure condition.
    5. Don’t be ashamed of dizziness and inform the Doctors or volunteers as soon as possible in case you aren’t feeling right.
    A day of strict diet can save several lives. Its healthy and its safe. So what’s your excuse this time?
